# **Little Book Club Brand Guidelines v1.0**

## **Brand Overview**

### **Brand Name**

Little Book Club

### **Tagline**

Adventures for today. Readers for life.

### **Mission**

Helping families build home libraries children love.

### **Brand Promise**

Affordable, carefully curated pre-loved children's books that make it easier for families to raise lifelong readers.

### **Positioning**

Little Book Club is not a traditional bookstore.

We do not sell individual books.

We help families build home libraries through curated bundles of quality pre-loved English-language children's books.

We are selling reading experiences, discovery, and a love of books.

### **Core Audience**

Primary Customer:

* Parents of children aged 0–12  
* Families living in Thailand  
* English-speaking and bilingual households  
* Parents who value reading, learning, and education  
* Parents seeking quality books at affordable prices

Secondary Audience:

* Grandparents  
* Relatives buying gifts  
* International families  
* Teachers and schools

---

# **Brand Personality**

Little Book Club should always feel:

* Warm  
* Friendly  
* Encouraging  
* Trustworthy  
* Curious  
* Community-focused  
* Optimistic  
* Helpful  
* Family-oriented

We are educational without feeling academic.

We are premium without feeling expensive.

We are playful without being childish.

---

# **What We Are Not**

Avoid appearing:

* Loud  
* Hyperactive  
* Cartoonish  
* Babyish  
* Overly academic  
* Corporate  
* Formal  
* Sales-driven

When a parent visits the website, the feeling should be:

"These people genuinely care about helping children become readers."

---

# **Brand Positioning Statement**

Little Book Club helps families build home libraries children love through affordable, carefully curated pre-loved books that inspire a lifetime of reading.

This statement should guide all design, marketing, content, and customer experience decisions.

---

# **Brand Pillars**

## **Reading First**

Reading is at the heart of everything we do.

Books are not products.

Books are opportunities, adventures, and memories.

---

## **Accessible Learning**

Every family should be able to afford quality books.

We make reading more accessible through carefully selected pre-loved books.

---

## **Trusted Curation**

Parents do not need to spend hours searching.

We select books families can feel confident bringing into their homes.

---

## **Sustainable Choice**

Giving books a second life is good for families and good for the planet.

Sustainability is a benefit, not the primary message.

---

## **Community**

Little Book Club is more than a store.

We are building a community of families who love books and reading.

---

# **Tone of Voice**

## **General Tone**

Write like a friendly, knowledgeable parent who loves books.

Warm.  
Conversational.  
Encouraging.

Never pushy.

---

## **We Sound Like**

* Helpful  
* Positive  
* Supportive  
* Friendly  
* Human  
* Honest

---

## **We Don't Sound Like**

* Corporate  
* Academic  
* Overly sales-focused  
* Trendy  
* Clever for the sake of being clever

---

## **Example**

Instead of:

"Purchase a curated collection of pre-owned children's literature."

Write:

"Discover a bundle of books your child will love."

---

Instead of:

"Improve literacy outcomes."

Write:

"Help your child fall in love with reading."

---

# **Colour Palette**

## **Primary Brand Colour**

### **Warm Book Orange**

HEX: \#F28C38

Purpose:

* Primary buttons  
* Key highlights  
* Icons  
* Calls to action  
* Brand accents

Orange should feel warm and welcoming.

Never aggressive or neon.

---

## **Secondary Colours**

### **Cream**

HEX: \#FFF8F0

Primary background colour.

Creates warmth and softness.

---

### **Soft Charcoal**

HEX: \#333333

Primary text colour.

Avoid pure black.

---

### **Sage Green**

HEX: \#7E9C72

Used for:

* Success messages  
* Sustainability references  
* Secondary accents  
* Informational highlights

---

### **Sky Blue**

HEX: \#A8D5E5

Used sparingly for:

* Feature highlights  
* Illustrations  
* Supporting accents

---

### **Light Sand**

HEX: \#F3E6D0

Used for:

* Card backgrounds  
* Section backgrounds  
* Soft contrast areas

---

# **Colour Usage Rules**

The brand should feel calm and uncluttered.

Recommended balance:

* 80% cream and white  
* 15% charcoal text  
* 5% accent colours

Orange should feel valuable and intentional.

Avoid large blocks of bright colour.

Avoid rainbow colour schemes.

Avoid children's-party aesthetics.

---

# **Typography**

## **Heading Font**

### **Baloo 2**

Used for:

* Main headings  
* Hero headings  
* Feature titles  
* Promotional highlights

Why:

* Friendly  
* Rounded  
* Memorable  
* Warm  
* Family-focused

---

## **Body Font**

### **Nunito**

Used for:

* Body copy  
* Product descriptions  
* Navigation  
* Forms  
* Emails

Why:

* Highly readable  
* Modern  
* Friendly  
* Works well at all sizes

---

## **Typography Principles**

Headings should feel warm and inviting.

Body text should prioritise readability.

Never use novelty fonts.

Never use handwriting fonts.

Never use Comic Sans.

---

# **Illustration Style**

## **Preferred Style**

Illustration-first brand.

Illustrations should be used in place of stock photography whenever possible.

---

## **Illustration Characteristics**

* Flat illustration style  
* Soft edges  
* Friendly shapes  
* Slightly hand-drawn feel  
* Modern and clean  
* Warm colour palette

---

## **Illustration Themes**

* Reading corners  
* Home libraries  
* Open books  
* Story adventures  
* Children reading  
* Families reading together  
* Bookshelves  
* Discovery and imagination

---

## **Avoid**

* Cartoon mascots  
* Disney-style characters  
* Anime-inspired styles  
* Overly detailed artwork  
* Busy illustrations

---

# **Iconography**

Icons should be:

* Rounded  
* Simple  
* Friendly  
* Consistent line weight  
* Easy to understand

Examples:

* Book  
* Bookshelf  
* Heart  
* Home  
* Reading lamp  
* Delivery box  
* Star  
* Recycling symbol  
* Magnifying glass

Avoid sharp, corporate icon sets.

---

# **Photography**

Photography should be limited.

If photography is used:

* Natural lighting  
* Real families  
* Real books  
* Calm environments  
* Cozy reading moments

Avoid:

* Overly posed stock photography  
* Classroom environments  
* Excessive smiling-at-camera shots

Illustration remains the preferred visual style.

---

# **Website Design Principles**

The website should feel:

* Cozy  
* Trustworthy  
* Organised  
* Family-friendly  
* Reading-focused

The experience should resemble a welcoming home library.

Not a toy store.

Not a discount retailer.

Not a school.

---

# **Design Style**

Use:

* Generous white space  
* Rounded corners  
* Soft shadows  
* Clean layouts  
* Clear hierarchy  
* Mobile-first design

Avoid:

* Clutter  
* Heavy animations  
* Bright colour overload  
* Complicated navigation

---

# **Emotional Goal**

When a parent visits Little Book Club, they should feel:

"This looks trustworthy."

"These books will be great for my child."

"I could see us buying from them regularly."

"I love the idea of building a home library."

---

# **Marketing Themes**

Content should regularly reinforce:

* Building a home library  
* Raising lifelong readers  
* Reading habits  
* Family reading time  
* Book discovery  
* Affordable books  
* Sustainability through reuse  
* Learning through stories

---

# **Core Brand Message**

Little Book Club exists to help families build home libraries children love.

Every page, email, social post, flyer, event, and customer interaction should reinforce that mission.

---

# **Brand Keywords**

Use these words as a final sense-check for all creative work:

Warm

Curious

Cozy

Trusted

Joyful

If a design, advert, email, or webpage does not feel like those five words, it should be revised.
