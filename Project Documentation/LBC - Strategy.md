# **Little Book Club**

## **Master Project Foundation Document**

---

# **Project Overview**

Little Book Club is a Thailand-based direct-to-consumer business selling curated bundles of quality pre-loved English-language children's books for ages 0–12.

The mission is to make English books more accessible and affordable for families while helping parents build home libraries and raise lifelong readers.

The business will initially operate online across Thailand, with school book fairs used as a customer acquisition and brand-building channel.

The business is intentionally designed to be:

* Low startup cost  
* Home-based  
* Low operational complexity  
* Highly scalable  
* Mission-driven  
* Family focused  
* Community oriented

Little Book Club is not intended to be a traditional bookstore.

We are not selling individual titles.

We are selling curated reading experiences and helping families build home libraries.

# **Core Business Thesis**

Little Book Club is not a second-hand book business.

It is a family reading brand that uses curated pre-loved books as the delivery mechanism.

The product is not books.

The product is:

* Building a home library  
* Encouraging reading habits  
* Supporting literacy development  
* Reducing screen time  
* Creating family reading experiences  
* Helping children become lifelong readers

Books are simply the tool that delivers those outcomes.

This distinction should influence every future decision relating to branding, marketing, pricing, website design, customer experience, school partnerships, content creation, and product development.

# **Inventory Strategy**

Little Book Club will not operate a detailed title-level inventory system.

The business model is intentionally simple.

Operational process:

Pallet arrives

→ Sort by reading level / age range

→ Sort by interest category

→ Store in crates, bins, or shelves

→ Build bundles to order

The objective is operational simplicity.

The business is not attempting to catalogue every individual book.

Books should be organised by:

1. Reading Level / Age Range  
2. Interest Category

Only basic operational tracking is required.

---

# **Reading Level Strategy**

Reading level is more important operationally than chronological age.

Customers may still see age ranges on the website.

However, internal sorting and curation should focus on reading appropriateness.

This reduces complexity and better reflects how children actually read.

Reading levels should broadly align to:

* 0–2  
* 3–5  
* 6–8  
* 9–12

---

# **Book History Tracking**

A lightweight customer book history system should be maintained.

Purpose:

Reduce duplicate books being sent to repeat customers.

Minimum data required:

Customer Record

Linked Books Received:

* Book Title  
* Author  
* Date Sent

This will be stored within the backend database.

No advanced inventory management is required.

A simple administration interface can be developed in the future.
